Toward the beginning of April, Sykes and Picot were named to go about as the central arbitrators yet again, this time on a month-long mission to the Middle East for further dialogs with the Sherif of Mecca and other Arab pioneers. On 3 April 1917, Sykes met with Lloyd George, Curzon and Hankey to get his guidelines in such manner, in particular to keep the French onside while "not prejudicing the Zionist development and the likelihood of its advancement under British protection, [and not] go into any political promises to the Arabs, and especially none concerning Palestine". Prior to going to the Middle East, Picot, through Sykes, welcomed Nahum Sokolow to Paris to instruct the French government on Zionism. Sykes, who had arranged the path in correspondence with Picot, arrived a couple of days after Sokolow; meanwhile Sokolow had met Picot and other French authorities, and persuaded the French Foreign Office to acknowledge for concentrate an announcement of Zionist points "as to offices of colonization, collective independence, privileges of language and foundation of a Jewish sanctioned organization." Sykes proceeded to Italy and had gatherings with the British represetative and British Vatican agent to set up the route for Sokolow by and by.
