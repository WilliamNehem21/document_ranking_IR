The Crusaders were trained in their first season by Vance Stewart, who was helped by Aussie Mclean. Stewart was supplanted by Wayne Smith in 1997; McLean too was supplanted, by Peter Sloane. Smith proceeded as mentor until he was delegated All Blacks mentor after the 1999 season. Robbie Deans assumed control over the reins for the 2000 season. Senior members has had a few partners: in 2000 and 2001 his right hand was Steve Hansen; in 2002, Colin Cooper; in 2003 and 2004, Don Hayes; and in 2005 and 2006, Vern Cotter. Senior members' colleague for the 2007 season was previous Crusader Mark Hammett. Senior members was selected mentor of Australia in December 2007, yet was permitted to keep training the Crusaders in the 2008 season. Previous skipper, Todd Blackadder was named his successor in July 2008, with Hammett proceeding as partner mentor. Hammett was named Hurricanes mentor from the earliest starting point of the 2011 season, and thus previous prop Dave Hewett was designated right hand advances mentor. In June 2016, Scott Robertson, previous Crusaders player and current head mentor of the Canterbury national common group and the New Zealand Under 20 rugby group, was reported as the Crusaders head mentor from 2017 through to 2019.
