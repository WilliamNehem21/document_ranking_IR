Luton Town Football Club was shaped on 11 April 1885. Prior to this there were numerous clubs in the town, the most unmistakable of which were Luton Wanderers and Luton Excelsior. A Wanderers player, George Deacon, concocted the possibility of a "Town" club which would incorporate all the best players in Luton. Drifters secretary Herbert Spratley seized upon Deacon's thought and orchestrated a mystery meeting on 13 January 1885 at the St Matthews school rooms in High Town.
