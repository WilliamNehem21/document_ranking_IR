Toward the beginning of March 1941, the second New Zealand Division (counting Div Cav) was reserved for W Force, the British Commonwealth constrain sent to Greece to reinforce its guard against unavoidable German attack. The regiment left for Greece on 18 March 1941 on the Greek ship "Ionia", with its vehicles on board the payload send "Old English Canadian". The boats landed at Piraeus on 21 March and the troops remained at the travel camp at Kifisia. The regiment propelled northward, achieving its situation on the Aliakmon Line close Katerini on 26 March. Its main goal was to annihilate the Aliakmon River spans, deferring the German development. E Troop of the fifth Field Regiment and O Troop of the seventh Anti-Tank Regiment were appended to the regiment. On 4 April, the regiment sent two troops of Marmon-Harrington defensively covered vehicles to fortify the British first Armored Brigade on the Macedonian plain; in return, the regiment got seven cruiser tanks. The two troops pulled back with the first Armored Brigade when it withdrew on 8 April and came to Perdikkas by the next day. The regiment and E Troop, fifth Field Regiment, were the main units left on the plain between Mount Olympus and the Aliakmon on 8 April. The following day, A Squadron obliterated the fundamental extension over the Aliakmon, after the first Armored Brigade had pulled back southward.
