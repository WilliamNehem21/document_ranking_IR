The railroad was approved in 1857 from Grange, on the Great North principle line, to Banff, with a branch from to . The administrator of the organization, Thomas Bruce, was additionally delegate executive of the Inverness and Aberdeen Junction Railway, with different chiefs being comprised of nearby men; the greater part of the speculations were brought locally and up in little sums. The vast majority of the line was developed with slopes to 1 of every 70, except the half-mile of 1 out of 30 merchandise line to the harbor at Portsoy was confined to one train and four wagons. The railroad opened on 30 July 1859, with open administrations beginning on 2 August after a crash on the opening day. Administrations associated with the Great North at Grange. With the railroad attempting to pay the enthusiasm on its obligation, in 1863 the Great North took over running the administrations and the line renamed the Banffshire Railway. The Great North given three trains every day among Grange and Banff that associated at Tillynaught for Portsoy, and two trains per day along the coast among Banff and Portsoy. Authorization for an augmentation from Portsoy to Portgordon was given, yet the fundamental speculation couldn't be found. Amalgamation with the Great North was approved in 1866, yet money related issues deferred this until 12 August 1867, and the Portgordon augmentation was surrendered.
