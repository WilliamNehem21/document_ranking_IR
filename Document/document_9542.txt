The understanding among Henry and Rhys was to last until Henry's demise in 1189. At the point when Henry's children defied him in 1173 Rhys sent his child Hywel Sais to Normandy to help the lord, at that point in 1174 by and by drove a military to Tutbury in Staffordshire to help at the attack of the fortification of the revolutionary Earl William de Ferrers. At the point when Rhys came back to Wales after the fall of Tutbury, he left a thousand men with the ruler for administration in Normandy. Lord Henry held a committee at Gloucester in 1175 which was gone to by a huge social affair of Welsh rulers, driven by Rhys. It seems to have finished up with the swearing of a common help agreement for the safeguarding of harmony and request in Wales. In 1177 Rhys, Dafydd abdominal muscle Owain, who had risen as the primary power in Gwynedd, and Cadwallon ap Madog from Rhwng Gwy a Hafren swore fealty and lord reverence to Henry at a board held at Oxford. At this chamber the ruler gave Meirionnydd, some portion of the kingdom of Gwynedd, to Rhys. There was some battling in Meirionnydd the next year, yet Rhys evidently made no genuine endeavor to add it.
