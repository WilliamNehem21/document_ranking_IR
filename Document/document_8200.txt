At the point when the letter was postponed at the Shaw Commission in 1929, Rustam Haidar addressed Faisal in Baghdad and cabled that Faisal had "no memory that he composed anything of the sort". In January 1930, Haidar kept in touch with a paper in Baghdad that Faisal: "discovers it exceedingly unusual that such an issue is credited to him as he at no time would consider enabling any remote country to partake in an Arab nation". Awni Abd al-Hadi, Faisal's secretary, wrote in his diaries that he didn't know that a gathering among Frankfurter and Faisal occurred and that: "I trust that this letter, accepting that it is bona fide, was composed by Lawrence, and that Lawrence marked it in English in the interest of Faisal. I trust this letter is a piece of the bogus cases made by Chaim Weizmann and Lawrence to lead off track popular assessment." According to Allawi, the undoubtedly clarification for the Frankfurter letter is that a gathering occurred, a letter was drafted in English by Lawrence, however that its "substance were not by any means clarified to Faisal.
