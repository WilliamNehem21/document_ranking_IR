On 1 March 1903, she turned into the leader of "KAdm" Gustav Schmidt, the representative officer of the I Squadron. That year, the squadron was busy with the ordinary peacetime routine of individual and unit preparing, incorporating a preparation journey in the Baltic, trailed by a voyage to Spain that kept going from 7 May to 10 June. In July, she set out on the yearly journey to Norway with the remainder of the squadron. The harvest time moves comprised of a bar practice in the North Sea, a voyage of the whole armada first to Norwegian waters and after that to Kiel toward the beginning of September, lastly a fake assault on Kiel. The activities closed on 12 September. The year's preparation plan closed with a journey into the eastern Baltic that began on 23 November and a voyage into the Skagerrak that started on 1 December. "Wittelsbach" and the I Squadron took an interest in an activity in the Skagerrak from 11 to 21 January 1904. Further squadron practices pursued from 8 to 17 March, and a noteworthy armada practice occurred in the North Sea in May. In July, the I Squadron and the I Scouting Group visited Britain, including a stop at Plymouth on 10 July. The German armada withdrew on 13 July, destined for the Netherlands; the I Squadron tied down in Vlissingen the next day. There, the boats were visited by Queen Wilhelmina. The I Squadron stayed in Vlissingen until 20 July, when they left for a journey in the northern North Sea with the remainder of the armada. The squadron halted in Molde, Norway, on 29 July, while different units went to different ports.
