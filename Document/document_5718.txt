"Distinguished" left Britain on 30 December and landed in Trincomalee, Ceylon, on 28 January 1944. She burned through the vast majority of the following a while preparing in spite of the fact that she took an interest in a few fights with the Eastern Fleet hunting down Japanese warships in the Bay of Bengal and close to the shore of Sumatra. The armada withdrew Trincomalee on 21 March to meet with the American bearer in anticipation of consolidated activities against the Japanese offices in the Dutch East Indies and the Andaman Islands. The main task did by the two transporters was an airstrike on the little maritime base at Sabang at the northern tip of Sumatra (Operation Cockpit). The transporter's air aggregate comprised of 21 Barracudas and 28 Corsairs for the task; "Renowned" propelled 17 of the previous escorted by 13 of the last on the morning of 19 April. The American planes assaulted the delivery in the harbor while the British air ship assaulted the shore establishments. The oil stockpiling tanks were annihilated and the port offices severely harmed by the Barracudas. There was no aeronautical restriction and the contenders professed to have crushed 24 flying machine on the ground. All British air ship returned securely albeit one American contender was compelled to discard amid the arrival home.
