Parliament held Charles under house capture at Holdenby House in Northamptonshire until Cornet George Joyce took him by risk of power from Holdenby on 3 June for the sake of the New Model Army. At this point, common doubt had created between Parliament, which favored armed force disbandment and presbyterianism, and the New Model Army, which was basically officered by congregationalist Independents, who looked for a more prominent political job. Charles was anxious to misuse the broadening divisions, and clearly seen Joyce's activities as an open door as opposed to a danger. He was taken first to Newmarket, at his very own recommendation, and after that exchanged to Oatlands and accordingly Hampton Court, while all the more at last vain arrangements occurred.
