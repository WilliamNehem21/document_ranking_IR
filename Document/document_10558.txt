Love the Chase originates from old and regarded stock, and California Chrome was the fifth Kentucky Derby victor delivered from this female horse line. Her sire, Not for Love, was by Mr. Miner and out of a little girl of Northern Dancer. Northern Dancer shows up again on the distaff side of Love the Chase's family. Her granddam, Chase the Dream, was sired by the 1968 Epsom Derby victor Sir Ivor. Enigmatically Noble, champ of the 1968 Prix de l'Arc de Triomphe, is one of Chase the Dream's grandsires. She follows to Princequillo and to the UK-reared Ribot, seen by some as the best racehorse of his age. Love the Chase has two crosses to the horse Numbered Account, who created a few Grade I stakes victors and was the American Champion Two-Year-Old Filly in 1971. Numbered Account was a little girl of Buckpasser, who earned five Eclipse Awards somewhere in the range of 1965 and 1967, and was accepted to the Horse Racing Hall of Fame in 1970. The Buckpasser line has been viewed as another great bloodline to crossbreed with relatives of Seattle Slew. Numbered Account was additionally a granddaughter of Swaps, and follows to La Troienne and War Admiral on the two sides of her family. Love the Chase's tail-female line is one of the most established in North America. Through a horse named Selima, foaled in 1745, who was by the Godolphin Arabian and imported to the Province of Maryland somewhere in the range of 1750 and 1752. The line most likely follows to Thoroughbred family 21, which started with the Moonah Barb Mare, who was imported from Barbary to England "in utero" around 1700.
