Positioned lieutenant in the Permanent Military Force (PMF), Pollard was delegated assistant/officer of the seventeenth Battalion (Citizens Military Forces), headquartered at North Sydney, in July 1925. He wedded Daisy Ethel Potter, a typist, at St Andrew's Anglican Church, Strathfield, on 31 October; "The Bathurst Times" revealed that Daisy cut the cake with her significant other's Sword of Honor. Pollard left for India on connection to the British Army in September 1927, presenting with the Royal Fusiliers and the York and Lancaster Regiment. He came back to Sydney in November the next year and was posted as assistant/officer to, progressively, the eighteenth Battalion (CMF) and, in September 1932, the 44th Battalion (CMF). In December 1932, while presenting with the 44th in Western Australia, he was elevated to skipper. Pollard was camp commandant of the National Rifle Association of Western Australia from 1934 to 1936. He was exchanged to Army Headquarters, Melbourne, in October 1936.
