The Royal Family utilized the line from 1853 to venture out to Balmoral Castle; in September 1866 the British Royal Train utilized Ballater station about a month prior to open administrations achieved the station. At first Queen Victoria visited once every year, this winding up two times per year after Albert kicked the bucket in 1861. The quantity of visits came back to one every year after Edward VII moved toward becoming lord in 1901. From 8 October 1865 a day by day 'Envoy Train' ran when the Royal Family was at Balmoral. Top notch convenience was accessible on these trains; going with hirelings were charged second rate class passages. In the late 1850s and mid 1860s the Great North and the Scottish North Eastern Railway (SNER) were in struggle over the joint station in Aberdeen. Disappointed with absence of advancement, the SNER proposed another line that crossed the Deeside Railway. While in discourses with the SNER about a connection from this new line to the Deeside, a rent for the Deeside Railway was offered to the Great North, which was quickly acknowledged. The Deeside board acknowledged the rent by a lion's share vote on 13 May 1862, and it was endorsed by Parliament on 30 July 1866. The Aboyne and Braemar stayed autonomous, despite the fact that administrations were worked by the Great North.
