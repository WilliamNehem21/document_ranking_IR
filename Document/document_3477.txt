After his appearances in London Solti went to Switzerland to search out Toscanini, who was directing in Lucerne. Solti trusted that Toscanini would help discover him a post in the US. He was unfit to do as such, however Solti looked for some kind of employment and security in Switzerland as vocal mentor to the tenor Max Hirzel, who was learning the job of Tristan in Wagner's musical drama. During the Time World War, Solti stayed in Switzerland. He didn't see his dad once more: Mor Stern passed on of diabetes in a Budapest emergency clinic in 1943. Solti was brought together with his mom and sister after the war. In Switzerland he couldn't acquire a work grant as a conductor, however earned his living as a piano educator. After he won the 1942 Geneva International Piano Competition he was allowed to give piano presentations, yet was as yet not permitted to lead.
