On 16 June, the Div Cav was requested to move to Egypt with the remainder of the division. The hostile by General Erwin Rommel had made a requirement for more troops to stop the Axis advance. The regiment landed at Matruh ten days after the fact, where it was to cover the northern and eastern pieces of the minefields around the town. Div Cav was the remainder of the divisional units to touch base back in Egypt. At the point when the division left Matruh on 26 June, the regiment's tanks and transporters had not yet arrived. B Squadron was outfitted with bearers and transport accessible in Matruh and was sent ahead to Garawla, losing two transporters to tanks en route and part up in the haziness. Half went to Minqar Qaim, where the fourth and fifth New Zealand Infantry Brigades were situated, and the other half, driven by Major Sutherland, laagered in a sadness and was encompassed by German tanks. B Squadron was then sent to Bir Khalda to supplant the 21st Battalion, watching there for the remainder of the day. Before dinnertime the division was almost encompassed, so acting division officer Brigadier Inglis settled on a breakout to Fortress An (otherwise called the Kaponga Box) south-west of El Alamein. B Squadron withdrew south along the Qattara Depression before swinging north to the stronghold, rejoining the regiment on 30 June.
