One of the family's quickest clients was Lieutenant-Colonel Thomas James Birch, later Bosvile, a rich gatherer from Lincolnshire, who purchased a few examples from them. In 1820 Birch wound up bothered by the family's neediness. Having made no significant revelations for a year, they were at the purpose of pitching their furnishings to pay the lease. So he chose to sell the fossils he had acquired from them for their sake. He kept in touch with the scientist Gideon Mantell on 5 March that year to state that the deal was "to assist the poor lady and her child and little girl at Lyme, who have in truth found nearly "all" the fine things which have been submitted to logical examination ... I may never again have what I am going to part with, yet in doing it I will have the fulfillment of realizing that the cash will be very much connected."
