There has for quite some time been banter about the thermoregulation of dinosaurs, based on whether they were ectotherms ("heartless") or endotherms ("warm-blooded"). Well evolved creatures and feathered creatures are homeothermic endotherms, which produce their own body heat and have an elevated capacity to burn calories, while reptiles are heterothermic ectotherms, which get their body heat from their environment.
