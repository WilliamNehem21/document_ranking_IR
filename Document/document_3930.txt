Secretariat dashed in Meadow Stables' blue-and-white-checkered hues. He never dashed in track gauzes, yet normally wore a signal hood, for the most part to enable him to center, yet in addition since he tended to keep running in towards the rail amid races. In January 1972, he joined mentor Lucien Laurin's winter stable at Hialeah. Secretariat picked up a notoriety for being a caring steed, amiable and unruffled in groups or by the knocking that happens between youthful ponies. He had the build of a sprinter, yet at first was cumbersome and awkward. He was as often as possible outpaced by progressively gifted stable mates, running a quarter-mile in 26 seconds contrasted with 23 seconds by his friends. His ordinary exercise riders were Jim Gaffney and Charlie Davis. Davis was not at first awed. "He was a huge sucker", Davis said. "That is to say, he was enormous. He wasn't in a rush to do nothin'. He took as much time as is needed. The quality was there, yet he didn't indicate it until he needed to."
